Executive Summary

A Trustworthy Answer Engine For Schools

Ed-Copilot gives students, parents, and teachers grounded, cited answers about curriculum standards and district policy — tailored to who is asking.

What

Grounded Q&A Assistant

Retrieval-Augmented Generation over each district's real curriculum and policy documents.

Who

3 Personas, 3 Districts

Students, parents, and teachers across Frisco ISD, Plano ISD, and Wake County NC.

Why It Matters

Fewer Wrong Answers

Every claim is cited back to source — reducing confusion, misinformation, and support load.

Why This Exists

The Business Case

Today, answers about curriculum and policy live in scattered PDFs and websites, or in generic chatbots that hallucinate district-specific facts.

Families and staff spend real time hunting through PDFs and district websites for answers that should take seconds to find.
Generic AI chatbots answer confidently but inaccurately when asked about a specific district's rules — creating risk, not clarity.
A grounded, cited, persona-aware assistant reduces support burden and builds trust in the information provided.

Business Requirements

Trust, Transparency & Compliance

Trust requirements govern how the assistant presents information sourced from third parties.

  • Every policy answer must disclose that content was scraped from public district websites and should be verified there.
  • The app must display a learning-purposes disclaimer, directing users to official district sources for authoritative information.
  • No answer may be presented without a traceable source citation when the intent is curriculum or policy related.
  • User feedback (ratings) is logged with context but without collecting personally identifying information.

    Technical Requirements

    Architecture Constraints

    Design decisions that shape how the system is built and extended.

    Plugin-Based Multi-Tenancy

    A district registry auto-discovers agents from config/tenants/*.yaml at startup — the orchestrator never hardcodes district logic.

    Graph-Based Orchestration

    LangGraph routes each request through intent classification to the correct district agent node, with a dedicated out-of-scope path.

    Hybrid Retrieval Required

    Dense + sparse search must be fused and reranked — single-method retrieval is insufficient for citation-grade accuracy.

    Technical Requirements

    Data & Retrieval Requirements

    Requirements governing how source documents are indexed and retrieved.

    RequirementDetail
    Chunking by standardCurriculum documents are split by standard ID (e.g. NC.M1.A-APR.1) so citations map to a precise, checkable unit.
    Dense + sparse fusionChromaDB dense search (BAAI/bge-small-en-v1.5) combined with BM25 keyword search via Reciprocal Rank Fusion.
    RerankingA cross-encoder (ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2) reranks fused candidates down to the top 4 highest-signal chunks.
    Incremental ingestionDelta checks prevent re-indexing already-ingested sources on repeated ingestion runs.
    Freshness metadataEvery ingestion run is logged with a timestamp and per-district chunk counts, surfaced in the UI.

    Workflow

    Document Ingestion Workflow

    How raw curriculum and policy documents become searchable, cited knowledge.

    1
    Parse & Chunk
    PyMuPDF extracts text; a regex chunker splits content by standard ID or policy section.
    2
    Check for Duplicates
    Already-indexed sources are skipped, so repeated ingestion runs stay incremental.
    3
    Embed
    New chunks are embedded locally with BAAI/bge-small-en-v1.5.
    4
    Store & Log
    Chunks are written to ChromaDB; the run is recorded in the ingestion log with timestamp and counts.
